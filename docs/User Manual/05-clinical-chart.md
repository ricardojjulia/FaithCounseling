# 05 — Clinical Chart

**Faith Counseling User Manual**

---

## Overview

The Clinical Chart is the primary documentation surface for counselors. It provides structured, focused spaces for session notes, internal notes, treatment plans, progress tracking, and homework assignments — all organized within a single client-specific workspace.

---

## 5.1 Opening a Clinical Chart

From the Clients list, click a client to open their detail page, then click **Clinical Chart** in the header or navigate to the **Chart** action from the Counselor Home quick actions.

The Clinical Chart opens to the client's chart summary and then tabs into specific documentation areas.

---

## 5.2 Chart Summary Header

At the top of the chart, the **Summary Header** displays:

- Client name, status, and primary diagnosis
- Assigned counselor
- Last session date
- Note-gap indicator (days since last note)
- Outstanding homework or treatment plan items

---

## 5.3 Session Notes Tab

The Session Notes tab is the primary place for documenting counseling sessions.

### Creating a Session Note

1. Click **Add Session Note**.
2. Confirm the **session date** and **appointment** (links to a scheduled appointment when available).
3. Enter detailed session content in the **note body** (rich text editor).
4. Include or reference clinical observations, client statements, interventions, and response.
5. Set the **note status**: Draft or Signed.
6. Click **Save**.

### Note Status

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Draft** | Note is in progress, not yet finalized |
| **Signed** | Note is finalized and part of the clinical record |

> **Best practice:** Sign notes promptly after each session. Unsigned notes contribute to note-gap compliance flags on the Dashboard.

### Editing and Viewing Notes

- Past notes appear in reverse-chronological order.
- Click any note to expand or edit it.
- Signed notes may be locked from editing depending on practice policy.

---

## 5.4 Internal Notes Tab

Internal Notes are clinical observations, caseload management notes, or staff-facing notes that are **not** part of the shareable client record.

### Use Cases

- Supervision notes
- Care coordination reminders
- Administrative tracking tied to a client

### Creating an Internal Note

1. Click **Add Internal Note**.
2. Enter the note content.
3. Click **Save**.

Internal notes are visible only to staff with chart access — they are never surfaced in the client portal.

---

## 5.5 Treatment Plan Tab

The Treatment Plan documents the structured care plan developed collaboratively between counselor and client.

### Treatment Plan Contents

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | Measurable care goals for this client |
| **Objectives** | Specific, time-bound steps toward each goal |
| **Interventions** | Counseling approaches and techniques to be used |
| **Estimated Duration** | Projected length of the treatment engagement |
| **Review Date** | When the plan is next scheduled for review |
| **Faith Integration Notes** | Any faith-aware or spiritually informed care elements incorporated into the plan |

### Creating a Treatment Plan

1. Click **Add Treatment Plan** (or **Edit** if one already exists).
2. Fill in goals, objectives, and interventions.
3. Optionally add faith integration notes.
4. Click **Save**.

### Reviewing and Updating

Treatment plans should be reviewed at regular intervals. Update the **Review Date** each time the plan is revisited.

---

## 5.6 Progress Tab

The Progress tab tracks the client's movement toward treatment goals over time.

### Progress Entries

Each progress entry includes:

- **Date** — when progress was noted
- **Goal reference** — which treatment plan goal this entry tracks
- **Progress rating** — a scale indicator of progress
- **Narrative** — counselor notes on observable progress or setbacks

### Adding a Progress Entry

1. Click **Add Progress Entry**.
2. Select the relevant goal.
3. Rate progress and add narrative.
4. Click **Save**.

Progress entries create a visible longitudinal record visible in both the chart and the [Faithful Workflows](06-faithful-workflows.md) engine.

---

## 5.7 Homework Tab

The Homework tab documents assignments given to clients between sessions.

### Homework Entries

Each homework entry includes:

- **Assignment date** — when the homework was given
- **Due date** — when completion is expected
- **Description** — what the client is asked to do
- **Status** — Assigned, Completed, Incomplete
- **Review notes** — counselor notes after reviewing client's response

### Assigning Homework

1. Click **Add Homework**.
2. Describe the assignment.
3. Set due date.
4. Click **Save**.

### Reviewing Homework

After the client completes (or does not complete) homework, update the status and add review notes for the clinical record.

---

## 5.8 Faith-Integrated Charting

Faith Counseling preserves counselor clinical judgment throughout all chart surfaces. Faith-aware elements appear in the treatment plan and can be referenced in session notes, but:

- Spiritual interventions are **chosen by the counselor**, not automated
- Faith content fields are **optional** in all chart tabs
- The Faith Profile on the client record provides the context for the counselor's own judgment
