# FaithCounseling — Competitive Analysis

**Date:** 2026  
**Scope:** Mental health practice management / EHR SaaS market, with focus on faith-based positioning and solo/group practice segment

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The mental health EHR market is large, mature, and dominated by a small number of well-funded general-purpose platforms. **No dedicated Christian counseling practice management platform exists at commercial scale.** Every mainstream competitor treats faith integration as a non-issue. This represents FaithCounseling's primary and unchallenged differentiator: it is the only purpose-built practice management platform for Christian counseling practices.

FaithCounseling enters an adjacent market (general mental health EHR) rather than a true faith-specific one. Its competitive strategy must therefore be **niche dominance first** — capture the underserved Christian counseling segment before feature-competitive generalist platforms add superficial faith content.

---

## 2. Market Overview

The U.S. mental health software market is estimated at several billion dollars annually and growing rapidly, fueled by post-pandemic behavioral health demand. Key market dynamics:

- **Solo to group practice segment** is largest by unit count; most software is priced per clinician per month.
- **Insurance reimbursement and billing automation** are the primary purchasing drivers for private practices.
- **Telehealth integration** became table stakes post-2020.
- **AI-assisted documentation** is the current high-growth feature area (2024–2026).
- **Faith/Christian counseling** is an underserved niche with no commercial EHR entrant.

## 3. Competitor Profiles

### 3.1 SimplePractice — Market Leader

**Company:** SimplePractice, LLC (Santa Monica, CA). Backed by private equity; major market position.  
**Market claim:** 250,000+ practitioners, 20 million+ clients.  
**URL:** simplepractice.com

**Feature Set:**
| Category | Features |
|---|---|
| Documentation | Customizable progress notes, AI-powered Note Taker (add-on), Wiley Treatment Planners®, diagnostic & treatment plans |
| Scheduling | Calendar management, conflict detection, calendar sync, online booking (client-initiated appointment requests) |
| Billing | Online payments, electronic claim filing, insurance eligibility, automated insurance status checks, superbills |
| Client engagement | Paperless intake, secure messaging, Client Portal (web + mobile app), appointment reminders |
| Clinical | Measurement-based care (outcome tracking), group appointments, group telehealth |
| Telehealth | Built-in video sessions (all plans) |
| Practice growth | Analytics & reports, team management, professional website builder, marketing tools |
| Mobile | Full iOS and Android EHR apps |
| Compliance | HIPAA, HITRUST, PCI certified; BAA available |
| Add-ons | AI Note Taker ($35/mo), ePrescribe ($49/mo + $89 setup) |

**Pricing:**
| Plan | Price |
|---|---|
| Starter | $49/month |
| Essential | $79/month |
| Plus | $99/month |
| Additional clinicians (Plus) | From $74/clinician/month |

**Strengths:**
- Dominant brand awareness and market share in solo/group segment
- Full-featured platform (the most complete feature set in category)
- HITRUST + PCI in addition to HIPAA — strongest compliance posture of any competitor
- AI note taker differentiates in 2025–2026 market
- Free credentialing offer as acquisition hook
- Native mobile apps for both practitioners and clients
- Professional website builder extends value beyond EHR
- Large user community and extensive resources

**Weaknesses:**
- No faith or Christian counseling content or terminology
- Pricing escalates significantly for group practices at $74+/additional clinician
- AI Note Taker is an extra $35/month; not included in base plans
- ePrescribe is expensive at $49/month + $89 setup
- Designed for secular private practice — no concept of pastoral care, spiritual wellness, scripture, or Christian clinical frameworks
- Large platform — may feel impersonal for small Christian practices

**Faith Integration:** None. Completely absent.

**Strengths:**
- Strong clinical documentation templates, well-regarded by mental health professionals
- Simpler, more focused product than SimplePractice
- Competitive price point
- Good support reputation
- HIPAA compliant; BAA available

**Weaknesses:**
- No faith integration or content
- No native mobile apps
- Less polished UI than SimplePractice
- Fewer practice growth / marketing features

**Faith Integration:** None.

### 3.3 Therabill (a WebPT Company) — Billing-First Platform

**Company:** WebPT, Inc. (acquired Therabill in 2019). Phoenix, AZ.  
**URL:** therabill.com  
**Target:** Behavioral health and speech therapy practices; billing-forward.

**Feature Set:**
- Electronic claims (EDI, clearinghouse routing, no per-claim fees)
- Scheduling (calendar + Google Calendar sync)
- Documentation / EMR (customizable templates, secure fax)
- Appointment reminders (email, text, voice)
- Electronic Remittance Advice (ERA)
- Patient invoicing (balance and ledger formats)
- Integrated credit card processing
- Custom reports (aging, balances, payments)
- Unlimited non-provider staff logins
- Provider portals
- WebPT integration (for PT/OT practices)

**Pricing:** Not publicly listed; requires demo/quote.

**Strengths:**
- Strong billing and claims automation — best-in-class for revenue cycle
- No per-claim fees differentiated in market
- Unlimited non-provider logins (better for larger billing teams than SimplePractice)
- Now part of WebPT ecosystem — enterprise backing

**Weaknesses:**
- Billing-first design; clinical documentation is secondary
- Less emphasis on client experience (portal, intake, engagement)
- No telehealth
- No AI features
- No mobile app
- No faith integration
- Not purpose-built for counseling/therapy (also targets speech therapy)

**Faith Integration:** None.

- Insurance claims operations team

**Pricing:**
- $95/month membership (billed annually at $1,140/year)
- Includes all EHR tools and network membership

**Strengths:**
- Insurance credentialing support is a major differentiator — removes a significant burden
- Community of 25,000+ practitioners for referrals and peer support
- AI notes included in base membership price

**Weaknesses:**
- Not a standalone EHR — requires full Alma ecosystem buy-in
- Practitioners must operate within Alma's network model
- No faith integration whatsoever
- $95/month is competitive with SimplePractice Plus
- EHR tools less comprehensive than SimplePractice or TherapyNotes
- No telehealth built-in
- No church or ministry channel

**Faith Integration:** None.

## 7. Go-to-Market Differentiation

### Primary differentiator
**FaithCounseling is the only practice management platform built specifically for Christian counselors.** Every other platform in the market is completely secular. This is a defensible moat that cannot be quickly copied without alienating secular customer bases.

### Target customer profile (where competition is weakest)
- Solo LPC, LPCC, LMFT, or pastoral counselor operating a private Christian practice
- Group practices at churches, Christian nonprofit counseling centers, or faith-based clinics
- Counselors who integrate scripture, prayer, and spiritual formation into their clinical model
- Supervisors overseeing interns under a Christian counseling framework

### Channels where mainstream competitors do not compete
- Church ministry networks and associations (e.g., AACC — American Association of Christian Counselors, 50,000+ members)
- Christian universities with counseling programs (seminary referral pipeline)
- Faith-based nonprofit counseling organizations
- Ministry conferences and Christian professional events
- Church administrator referral networks

### Positioning statement
> *FaithCounseling is the practice management platform designed for Christian counselors — the only EHR that integrates faith language, spiritual wellness tracking, scripture references, and Christian counseling templates into every aspect of your practice workflow.*

## 9. Strategic Recommendations

### Immediate (v1 launch)
1. **Lead with faith differentiation.** Every marketing touchpoint must make clear that FaithCounseling was built for Christian counselors — not adapted for them.
2. **Target AACC membership.** American Association of Christian Counselors has 50,000+ members. Conference sponsorship or partnership would reach the entire addressable market.
3. **Price at or slightly below SimplePractice Essential.** Competing at $69–79/month for full features undercuts the market leader while the product builds feature parity.
4. **Launch with a 30-day free trial.** Category standard; required for credibility.

### Near-term (v1.x — post-launch)
5. **Add telehealth.** This is the largest functional gap vs. market standard. Even a basic HIPAA-compliant video link integration (e.g., Zoom for Healthcare, Doxy.me) closes the most common objection.
6. **Build out faith content library.** Pre-built templates for Christian CBT, spiritually integrated intake, faith-based treatment goals. This deepens the moat that competitors cannot easily copy.
7. **Certification content.** Create HIPAA BAA documentation and compliance checklist; position the platform as BAA-ready from day one.

### Medium-term (v2+)
8. **Church and ministry plan.** Bundle counseling platform with church integration hooks (ministry directory, referral source tracking, care team coordination) — a feature set no competitor can offer.
9. **AI session notes.** AI-assisted progress note drafting is becoming a market expectation; plan this before it becomes a disqualifier.
10. **AACC directory integration.** List practices in a find-a-counselor directory native to the platform; creates network effects within the faith counseling community.
